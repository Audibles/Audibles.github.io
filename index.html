SLA Dashboard

I created the SLA Dashboard as a part of my internship project while working at EA. This dashboard displays key metrics for the services that run the behind-the-scenes parts of EA's games, such as the servers and ingame stores. The dashboard also allows EA team members to diagnose errors and response time issues within a particular service that may be affecting a certain game's playability or causing players to experience downtime. I designed and implemented this site based on the needs of my team, working from an existing team codebase to add additional pages and components.

  • Languages/Skills: HTML, CSS, JavaScript, ReactJS, Redux, Balsamiq
  • Date: Summer 2017
  • Company Website: EA (Electronic Arts)

Witch Garden?

Witch Garden? is a game where you are a witch who is extremely angry due to the fact that she has lost the annual "Witch Garden?" contest for the umpteenth time. Her solution is to go on a mad rampage, destroying all the other witches' gardens to the best of her ability without being caught by their magical traps and guards. You control the witch and destroy the garden terrain with physical attacks and magic spells while stealthing around. I created this game in a group with three other friends in Unity as a final project in a game development class.

  • Languages/Skills: Unity, C#
  • Date: Spring 2017
  • Club Website: GameCraft at UC Berkeley

Berkeley Maps

The Maps project was a project I completed as a part of my computer science class CS61A at UC Berkeley. In this project I worked with a partner to write an algorithm that analyzes restaurants and user reviews around the Berkeley campus and creates a map that shows the user where they should go to find a restaurant they would like most. The different colored areas of the map indicate how close the user is to a restaurant that fits their tastes, and the dots are the actual restaurants. I coded this project in Python.

  • Languages/Skills: Python
  • Date: Fall 2016
  • Class Website: CS61A at UC Berkeley
